Retention Is Not a Team Problem
It's an Architecture Problem

We unify content strategy, instructional design, and creative production to build education systems that connect every team and every customer touchpoint.

Evolution of Buyer Behaviors

B2B buyers now discover, research and evaluate products in entirely new ways.

Analog Buying Era

Previously
  • — Fewer sources of information
  • — Buyers saw peers once or twice per year at conferences and tradeshows
  • — Sellers could control the flow of information through planned interactions
  • — Buyers spent more time with vendor representatives gathering info they now obtain through 3rd party sources

Website Era

Access to Information

New Dynamics:

  • — Access to endless, always-on information across the internet
  • — Ability to complete the entire buying process/ evaluation independently
  • — Buying decisions are based on evolving criteria, wider set of stakeholders, and are more independent than ever

Dark Social Era

Access to Peers
  • — Hyper connection of B2B peers through digital channels like social networks, communities, and word-of-mouth has scaled education, advocacy, sharing and consumption
  • — Today's buyer does their own research using trusted peers and prefers to remain anonymous until ready to start a sales conversation
  • — Today's buyer is often the customer, where user-led decisions control the buying experience: emotions, motivations, and peer influence plays a larger role
Market Reality

Retention Now Starts Before the Sale

Expectations are formed before onboarding ever begins — and those expectations determine whether customers stay.

Buyers now learn in the wild—on LinkedIn, YouTube, Slack communities, dark social, and self-guided research.

They talk to a rep last.

They judge your product long before they log into it.

And they churn the moment friction outweighs value.

The System Challenge
